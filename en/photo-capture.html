How to use it

  1. Click "Start Camera." Grant access and the video shows up.
  2. Frame your shot. You're ready once the corner brackets turn green.
  3. Click "Shutter." That exact frame gets captured as a still.
  4. Hit "Save" to download the PNG, or "Retake" if you're not happy with it.
Your captured image only exists in the browser's memory. Reload or close the page and it's gone unless you saved it.

Tips for a better shot

You can find these tips anywhere, but after a few hundred test shots with this tool, the one thing that consistently mattered most was light.

  • Line the camera up with your face, at eye level.
  • Put the light source in front of you or to the side — not behind you (avoid backlighting). Get this right and it papers over most of the gap between a cheap and an expensive camera.
  • If the background is cluttered, shoot in front of a plain wall.
  • Check your camera's max resolution ahead of time with the Resolution Test so you're not surprised by a grainier shot than expected.

Frequently asked questions

Is my photo uploaded anywhere?

No. From capture to download, everything happens inside the browser tab where this page is open.

What if the photo looks grainy or blurry?

Try brightening the room, or check your camera's max resolution on the Camera Info page. Many built-in cameras cap out at 720p, and an external camera can often do better.

Can I take several photos at once?

Right now it's one photo at a time. If you need a burst, just press the shutter repeatedly and save each one as you go.
