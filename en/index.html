How to use the webcam test

  1. Click "Start Camera." Your browser will prompt for camera access.
  2. Choose "Allow." Video appears in the viewfinder and the four corner brackets turn green.
  3. Check the readout. Resolution, frame rate, and brightness update live.
  4. Turn on the grid if you need it. "Show Grid" overlays rule-of-thirds lines for framing.
Video and audio are never sent to a server. Everything is processed inside your browser on this device, and it's discarded the moment you close the page.

How to read each metric

Resolution

The actual pixel count your camera is streaming. 720p (1280×720) or higher is plenty for a video call; 1080p (1920×1080) gives you room for recording or streaming too. If the number comes in surprisingly low, suspect another app hogging the camera or a USB bandwidth bottleneck.

Frame rate (FPS)

How many frames per second your camera is delivering. 24fps and up looks smooth; below 15fps, motion starts to look choppy. In low light, cameras often drop their frame rate automatically to gather more light per frame.

Brightness

An estimate of average luminance on a 0–255 scale. Under 40 is too dark to see clearly; over 230 means you're blown out. Aim for 80–200 by adjusting your desk lamp or window position. Watch out for backlighting — a window behind you can leave your face dark even when the overall brightness number looks fine. If the number looks good but the picture still looks off, check where your light source is first.

Frequently asked questions

Is my camera footage saved or sent anywhere?

No. This tool uses the browser's getUserMedia API and does all processing on your device. Video or photos are never uploaded to a server.

I clicked "Start Camera" but nothing shows up

In most cases, either camera access was previously blocked in the browser's permission dialog, or another app (Zoom, Teams, OBS, etc.) already has the camera open. See the troubleshooting guide for step-by-step fixes.

Do I need to install anything or sign up?

No. Just open this page — it works immediately in any modern browser with no extra software.

Does this work on smartphones?

Yes, it works on major mobile browsers like iOS Safari and Android Chrome. Some devices don't support switching between front and rear cameras — see the compatibility table below.
