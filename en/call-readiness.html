What's being tested

  • Camera — whether video can be captured, and whether it's unusually dark.
  • Microphone — whether there's audio input at all, checked with a live level meter. Say something out loud for a few seconds.
  • Speaker — whether a test tone actually reaches your speakers or headphones, confirmed by you (this can't be detected automatically).
  • Connection speed — an estimated download speed based on how long it takes to fetch a 2MB file from this site. A rough guide, not an official speed measurement.
Video and audio are processed only on this device and never sent anywhere. The connection speed check uses only a 2MB test file hosted by this site itself — no third-party service is contacted.

Frequently asked questions

The speed number doesn't match Zoom or Speedtest. Why?

This test is a quick, one-time download of a small (2MB) file hosted on this same site — a different method than large-scale speed test services use. Treat it as a rough check for whether your connection is obviously too slow, not an official measurement.

Why isn't the speaker test automatic like the others?

A browser has no way to detect whether sound actually reached your ears. That's why this plays a test tone and then asks you to confirm you heard it by clicking a button.
