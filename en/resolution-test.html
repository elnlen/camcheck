How this test works

The web standard getUserMedia lets you request an "ideal" resolution. This tool requests five steps from SD up to 4K, in order, and records exactly what the camera and driver hand back. It's common for that number to differ from the maximum resolution printed on the box.

Most built-in laptop cameras cap out at 720p. If you want reliable 1080p or better, an external USB webcam is worth considering.

How to read the results

  • Green (supported) — responded at the requested resolution, or close to it.
  • Yellow (approximate) — couldn't get the requested resolution; the camera fell back to its nearest supported value.
  • Red (unsupported) — access at that resolution failed outright.

Frequently asked questions

It says "4K" but I only get 1080p in practice

"4K" on the box often refers to the still-photo sensor resolution, not video streaming. Video streaming has its own, usually lower, cap — and driver or USB bandwidth limits can push it lower still.

Does the picture freeze briefly during the test?

The camera briefly reinitializes each time it switches resolution, so a fraction of a second to about a second of freeze is normal — it's not a malfunction.
