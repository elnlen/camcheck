Camera Info — See Every Connected Webcam

Open this page and it automatically detects the cameras attached to your device — device name, max resolution, and max frame rate, all in one list. If you're on the fence about buying a new external camera, these numbers alone are often enough to decide.

What this tool shows you

  • Device name — the label your OS assigns to the camera. Useful for telling multiple cameras apart.
  • Max resolution — the highest resolution the camera can theoretically output. What you actually stream depends on the app's own settings.
  • Max frame rate — the most frames per second it can output. Higher means smoother video.
  • Zoom / focus support — whether the camera exposes controls the browser can drive.
  • Preview — click "Preview This Camera" on any card to see that camera's actual feed. Handy when you can't tell which physical camera is which.
Some browsers anonymize the device name (e.g. "camera 1") until you've granted access. Click "Scan Cameras" and allow access once to see the real names.

If multiple cameras aren't being detected

Even with both a laptop's built-in camera and an external USB camera connected, browsers generally enumerate every device. If only some show up, check that your OS is allowing the browser access to the camera, and make sure no other app is holding the device exclusively. See the troubleshooting guide for more detailed steps.

Frequently asked questions

Why doesn't the device name show up at first?

For privacy, browsers hide the full device name until you grant camera access. Click "Scan Cameras" and allow access to see the real device name.

Can I use an external camera and the built-in one at the same time?

You can switch between devices in the list to test them individually, but as with most video call apps, only one can stream at a time.
