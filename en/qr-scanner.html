How to use it

  1. Click "Start Scanning." Grant camera access when prompted.
  2. Fit the QR code inside the frame. Use the corner brackets as a guide, and move close enough for it to be in focus.
  3. It's detected automatically. The moment it's read, the content appears — click "Open Link" if it's a URL.
Decoded QR code content only ever appears in this browser's memory on this device. It is never sent to or logged on a server.

If it won't scan

  • Adjust the distance between the QR code and the camera — too close or too far both hurt recognition.
  • Check for glare or shadows falling across the code.
  • Brighten the room if the whole scene is dark — you can check camera brightness with the Webcam Test.
  • Hold the camera or the code steady to reduce motion blur.

Frequently asked questions

Is it risky to open the URL it decodes?

A QR code is just an encoded string (a URL or text). Whether the destination is trustworthy is exactly the same question as with any web link — check the URL carefully before opening it.

Can I scan several QR codes in a row?

Scanning pauses after it reads one code. Click "Scan Again" to read another.

What kinds of QR codes are supported?

Standard QR content like URLs, plain text, and Wi-Fi config strings gets decoded and shown as-is. Contact (vCard) and Wi-Fi QR codes are also read as text, but their settings aren't applied automatically.
