Green Screen / Virtual Background Test

Start the camera with your own green (or blue) screen backdrop behind you, and that color gets keyed out and composited over a different background in real time. This is classic chroma key — it needs a solid-colored backdrop, not AI person detection. Light it properly and even a simple setup composites cleanly.

How to use it

  1. Set up a green (or blue) screen behind you. Pull it taut to avoid wrinkles and shadows.
  2. Click "Start Camera." You'll first see the unprocessed video.
  3. Match the key color. If the preset green/blue doesn't match, use "Pick From Video" and click your actual backdrop color.
  4. Adjust the threshold. Raise it if the background isn't fully keyed out; lower it if parts of you start disappearing.
  5. Choose a background. Transparent, solid color, or an image.
This isn't automatic AI person-cutout — it's classic chroma key that makes a chosen color transparent. The more uniform your backdrop color (an actual green or blue screen), the cleaner the key.

Tips for a cleaner key

  • Keep at least a meter between you and the backdrop to reduce color spill.
  • Light the backdrop evenly to avoid shadows and hot spots.
  • Avoid wearing clothing close to the key color (no green shirts with a green screen).
  • If lighting is weak, check brightness ahead of time with the Webcam Test.

Frequently asked questions

Can AI remove my background without a green screen?

This tool uses classic chroma key — it makes a chosen solid color transparent — rather than AI-based person segmentation, so it needs a plain backdrop. A solid-colored wall or curtain can sometimes stand in for one.

Can I use the composited video directly in a video call?

This page is for testing and previewing in your browser. To feed this composited output into a real call app (Zoom, etc.) as a virtual camera, you'd need to route it through separate broadcast software like OBS.

Does the background show up in the image saved by "Save This Frame"?

If the background mode is "Transparent," the keyed-out area is saved as a transparent PNG. With "Color" or "Image," it saves with your chosen background already composited in.
