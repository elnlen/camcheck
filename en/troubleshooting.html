● Troubleshooting

Webcam Not Working? Here's What to Try

The usual suspects are permission settings, another app holding the camera, drivers, or OS-level privacy settings. In practice, those four cover about 90% of cases. Work through them in order and most issues clear up in a few minutes. Start by reproducing the problem on the Webcam Test, then try the section that matches.

Checklist by symptom

1. Screen is black / nothing shows up

  • Check the camera icon near your browser's address bar to confirm this site is set to "Allow."
  • Make sure every other app that uses the camera — Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, OBS — is fully closed. Most systems only let one app use the camera at a time.
  • If your laptop's camera has a physical shutter or cover, make sure it's open.

2. The permission dialog never appears

Once you (or someone) selects "Block," the browser remembers that choice and won't ask again. Reset the per-site setting with these steps:

  • Chrome / Edge: Click the icon left of the address bar → "Site settings" → set Camera to "Allow" → reload the page.
  • Firefox: Click the shield or lock icon in the address bar → "Permissions" → clear the block.
  • Safari: Menu bar → "Safari" → "Settings for This Website" → set Camera to "Allow."

3. The camera doesn't show up in the list / isn't detected

  • For USB cameras, unplug and reconnect, and try a different port.
  • Check Device Manager (Windows) or System Information (macOS) to confirm the OS sees the camera at all.
  • Permissions sometimes reset right after an OS update — double-check the OS settings below.

4. Picture is grainy / resolution is low

Check what resolution it actually supports with the Resolution Test. Most built-in cameras cap out at 720p. If USB bandwidth is tight, temporarily unplugging other USB devices can help.

5. Choppy video / low frame rate

In low light, cameras often drop their frame rate automatically to compensate. Try brightening the room or aiming a desk lamp at your face. Heavy CPU load from other running tasks can also drag the frame rate down.

Privacy settings by OS

Windows 10 / 11

  1. Open "Settings" → "Privacy & security" → "Camera."
  2. Turn on "Let apps access your camera."
  3. Also turn on "Let desktop apps access your camera," and confirm your browser is in the allowed list.

macOS

  1. Open "System Settings" → "Privacy & Security" → "Camera."
  2. Make sure your browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, etc.) is checked.
  3. Some changes only take effect after restarting the browser.

Still not working?

At this point a hardware fault is worth suspecting. Try a different browser, a different USB port, and if possible a different computer entirely. If it fails everywhere, suspect the camera or its cable.

If none of this fixes it, check the exact error shown in the status message on the Webcam Test (NotAllowedError, NotFoundError, etc.) — that usually narrows down the cause quickly.
